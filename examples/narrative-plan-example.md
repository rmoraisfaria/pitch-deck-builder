# Narrative Plan Template - Example

This template shows the structure and detail level expected in a narrative plan. Use this as a reference when building your own deck.

---

## Project: Series A Pitch Deck for [Company Name]

**Deck Type**: Investor Pitch (Series A)  
**Audience**: VCs, potential lead investors  
**Goal**: Raise $5M Series A round  
**Length**: 15 slides  
**Timeline**: 10-minute presentation  
**Key Narrative**: How we've found product-market fit and are scaling our solution to capture a massive market opportunity.

---

## Slide-by-Slide Narrative Plan

### SLIDE 1: Cover
**Slide Title**: [Company Name] - [Tagline]

**Core Message**: 
Establish the company name and value proposition immediately. Set professional tone.

**Supporting Points**:
- Company name (prominent)
- One-line tagline capturing core value
- Visual element (logo or product visualization)
- Founder name and contact info
- Date/version

**Visual Strategy**: 
Full-bleed design with brand color background, company name in large sans-serif typography (48pt+), subtle product visual on right side, minimal text.

**Speaker Notes**: 
"Thanks for being here. We're [Company] and we're transforming [industry] by [core value]. I'm [Founder Name]."

**Data Requirements**: 
None - just company branding

---

### SLIDE 2: Problem
**Slide Title**: "Healthcare Providers Waste 15+ Hours Per Week on Administrative Burden"

**Core Message**: 
Healthcare providers are drowning in administrative work, with significant personal and financial costs.

**Supporting Points**:
- Average physician spends 35% of time on non-clinical administrative tasks
- Leading cause of burnout cited by 63% of doctors surveyed
- Costs the system $4.6B annually in lost productivity
- Patients receive less attention from overwhelmed providers

**Visual Strategy**: 
Large headline with supporting statistics beside a powerful visual (doctor overwhelmed by paperwork, or workflow diagram showing time waste). One striking data visualization showing the 35% time split.

**Speaker Notes**: 
"Every day, physicians are spending more time on paperwork than on patient care. One study found that doctors waste 15+ hours per week just managing administrative tasks. This doesn't just frustrate doctors—it leads to burnout and costs the healthcare system billions."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Burnout statistic: 63% (Source: Medscape Burnout Study)
- Time waste: 35% (Source: Journal of General Internal Medicine)
- Annual cost: $4.6B (Source: CDC/CMS data)

---

### SLIDE 3: Solution Overview
**Slide Title**: "We Automate Clinical Documentation, Freeing Doctors to Focus on Patients"

**Core Message**: 
Our AI-powered solution handles administrative burden so doctors can spend time on what matters.

**Supporting Points**:
- Real-time voice-to-text documentation during patient visits
- AI understands medical context and terminology
- Integrates seamlessly with existing EHR systems
- Reduces documentation time by 75%

**Visual Strategy**: 
Left side: Text with key benefits and 75% callout in large type. Right side: Product screenshot showing actual interface with voice-to-text in action.

**Speaker Notes**: 
"We built an AI that understands clinical context and can capture patient visits in real-time audio. It creates accurate, formatted documentation automatically—not generic transcription, but actual medical records ready to sign."

**Data Requirements**: 
- 75% time reduction (from internal testing)
- Integration capabilities (feature check)

---

### SLIDE 4: Product Demo
**Slide Title**: "DocFlow in Action: From Patient Visit to Signed Records in 90 Seconds"

**Core Message**: 
Show the actual product workflow—how simple and effective it is.

**Supporting Points**:
- Doctor starts visit (1 click)
- AI captures conversation in real-time
- System generates draft documentation
- Doctor reviews and signs (takes 15 seconds)
- Records sent to EHR

**Visual Strategy**: 
Sequence of 4-5 product screenshots showing the workflow from patient interaction to final documentation. Use arrows to show flow. Keep each screenshot clean and focused.

**Speaker Notes**: 
"Here's what it looks like in practice. Doctor starts the visit, the AI captures the conversation in real-time, and within 90 seconds we've created a complete, formatted medical record ready to sign. No dictation, no delays—it just works."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Actual product screenshots
- Workflow timings (from QA)

---

### SLIDE 5: Traction - Revenue Growth
**Slide Title**: "We've Tripled Revenue Quarter-Over-Quarter to $800K ARR"

**Core Message**: 
Strong growth trajectory demonstrates we've found product-market fit and have significant runway.

**Supporting Points**:
- Started at $200K ARR, now at $800K ARR (4X growth in 12 months)
- Consistent quarter-over-quarter growth (50%+ each quarter)
- Net negative churn (upsells exceeding any churn)
- Projected to hit $2.5M ARR by year-end

**Visual Strategy**: 
Line chart showing quarterly ARR growth with bold upward trajectory. Use brand color for the line. Add data labels at each point. Include a "Projected" dashed line extending to end of year. Large callout box showing "4X growth in 12 months" and "$800K ARR today."

**Speaker Notes**: 
"But the real proof is in our growth. We started with early customers last year at $200K ARR. We've now tripled that to $800K, with consistent 50% quarter-over-quarter growth. Based on our current sales pipeline, we're tracking to hit $2.5M by year-end."

**Data Requirements**: 
- ARR by quarter: Q1 $200K, Q2 $325K, Q3 $525K, Q4 $800K
- Growth rates and projections
- Churn/net retention data

---

### SLIDE 6: Traction - Customer Validation
**Slide Title**: "Leading Health Systems Trust DocFlow"

**Core Message**: 
Top-tier customers validate our solution and use case.

**Supporting Points**:
- 12 health systems using our platform
- Ranging from 50-bed regional hospitals to 500+ bed major medical centers
- Used in emergency, surgical, and outpatient settings
- Average customer reviews: 4.8/5 stars

**Visual Strategy**: 
Display 6-8 customer logos (actual logos, not names). Include a customer quote in a callout box: "DocFlow gave us our physicians back. They now spend 20% more time in patient care." - Dr. Sarah Chen, Chief Medical Officer, Regional Health System

**Speaker Notes**: 
"Our customers include some of the top health systems in the country. From emergency departments to surgical centers, teams are using DocFlow to reclaim time and reduce burnout. The feedback has been remarkable—our Net Promoter Score is 72, which is in the top tier for healthcare software."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Customer logos (with permission)
- Customer quote and attribution
- NPS score: 72
- Customer segment distribution

---

### SLIDE 7: Market Opportunity
**Slide Title**: "The $8B Ambulatory Documentation Market Is Ripe for Disruption"

**Core Message**: 
Massive, growing market with clear pathway to capture significant share.

**Supporting Points**:
- TAM (Total): $50B (entire healthcare IT market)
- SAM (Serviceable): $8B (ambulatory provider market in US)
- SOM (Obtainable): $400M (our 5-year target)
- Market growing 12% annually with AI acceleration

**Visual Strategy**: 
Nested circles (Venn diagram style) showing TAM/SAM/SOM relationship. Use concentric circles with clear labels. Add market growth arrow. On the right, bottom-up calculation: "2,000 target hospitals × $200K average deal = $400M"

**Speaker Notes**: 
"The ambulatory healthcare market is $50B globally, and the specific problem we're solving—documentation automation—is an $8B opportunity. We're not trying to capture all of healthcare IT; we're focused on the $400M to $800M of that representing our core target of mid-to-large health systems."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Market sizing research (sources: Gartner, IDC)
- Target hospital count: 2,000
- Average deal size: $200K
- Growth rate: 12% annually

---

### SLIDE 8: Business Model & Unit Economics
**Slide Title**: "Predictable SaaS Model with Exceptional Unit Economics"

**Core Message**: 
Sustainable, scalable business model with strong margins.

**Supporting Points**:
- $2-8K monthly per health system (based on size)
- 3-year implementation + annual renewals (95% net retention)
- Gross margin: 72% (Cloud hosting + support costs)
- CAC: $40K | LTV: $360K | LTV/CAC ratio: 9:1

**Visual Strategy**: 
Left side: Text with key metrics and LTV/CAC callout highlighted in brand color. Right side: Waterfall chart showing revenue → COGS → Gross Profit breakdown visually.

**Speaker Notes**: 
"We're a traditional SaaS model: customers pay monthly based on their size, typically $2-8K per month. Our implementation is fast—30-60 days—and our customer retention is exceptional at 95%. We have strong unit economics with a 9-to-1 LTV to CAC ratio, which means every dollar we spend on sales and marketing generates $9 in lifetime value."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Pricing: $2-8K monthly (average: $4.5K)
- Gross margin: 72%
- CAC: $40K (calculated from actual spend)
- LTV: $360K (based on $4.5K monthly × 84 months × 95% retention)

---

### SLIDE 9: Competition & Positioning
**Slide Title**: "We're the Only Solution Built Specifically for Ambulatory Providers"

**Core Message**: 
Clear differentiation in a market with larger but non-focused competitors.

**Supporting Points**:
- Epic/Cerner focus on hospital systems and full EHR replacement (high cost, slow implementation)
- Dragon Medical is dictation-only (no documentation generation)
- We focus exclusively on ambulatory providers with fast implementation and AI-native documentation

**Visual Strategy**: 
2x2 Matrix positioning:
- X-axis: Implementation Speed (slow → fast)
- Y-axis: AI Capability (Limited → Advanced)
- Our position: Top right (fast + advanced)
- Competitors scattered: Epic (slow + limited), Dragon Medical (fast + limited), Cerner (slow + limited)

**Speaker Notes**: 
"In this space, there are bigger companies but none focused specifically on ambulatory providers. Epic and Cerner built their systems 20 years ago and have built-in technical debt. Dragon Medical is basically glorified dictation. We're the first solution purpose-built for ambulatory using modern AI."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Competitive feature comparison
- Implementation timelines (from sales data)
- Customer feedback on speed of deployment

---

### SLIDE 10: Go-to-Market Strategy
**Slide Title**: "Strategic Sales Approach Targeting High-Value Health Systems"

**Core Message**: 
Clear, targeted go-to-market strategy with proven results.

**Supporting Points**:
- Focus on 500+ bed health systems first (highest value, best fit)
- Sales-led model with dedicated account executives
- Average sales cycle: 4-6 months (relatively fast for healthcare)
- Plan to expand to smaller systems after establishing market presence

**Visual Strategy**: 
Funnel chart showing sales process:
- Leads: 40 health systems contacted
- Qualified: 12 in active discussion
- Proposals: 5 proposals out
- Closed: 2 new customers this quarter

**Speaker Notes**: 
"We're not trying to be everything to everyone. We're focused on the 200+ largest health systems in the US, which represent 80% of the market value. Our sales team has deep healthcare relationships, and we're closing deals in 4-6 months, which is fast for healthcare IT."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Sales funnel metrics (actual pipeline)
- Average sales cycle length
- Target market size: 200+ bed health systems = 200 institutions
- Geographic expansion plan

David built NLP systems at [Company]. And Michael isn't just an advisor—he's a practicing physician using the product daily."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Founder bios and credentials
- Photo assets
- Prior company growth numbers
- Relevant expertise and achievements

### SLIDE 12: Financials & Projections
**Slide Title**: "Realistic Path to $25M ARR in 3 Years"

**Core Message**: 
Conservative yet ambitious growth projections based on clear assumptions.

**Supporting Points**:
- Year 1: $2M ARR (current trajectory)
- Year 2: $8M ARR (accelerate sales, expand footprint)
- Year 3: $25M ARR (market leadership position)
- Based on: 50 customers × $400K average contract value (conservative)

**Visual Strategy**: 
Line chart showing revenue growth with three elements:
1. Historical revenue (actual data from last 12 months)
2. Projected revenue (clear projection line from current to $25M)
3. Annotation showing key growth drivers and assumptions below

**Speaker Notes**: 
"Our projections are conservative. We're assuming we capture 50 health systems by year three—which would be about 25% of the major health systems market. Based on our current pricing and pipeline, that gets us to $25M ARR."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Historical revenue data (actual)
- Detailed assumptions: customer count growth, average contract value
- Bottom-up build (not just hockey stick)
- Unit economics verification

### SLIDE 13: The Ask & Use of Funds
**Slide Title**: "We're Raising $5M to Become the Market Leader"

**Core Message**: 
Clear funding ask with specific use of funds and milestones.

**Supporting Points**:
- Raising $5M Series A
- Use: 60% Engineering (scale platform), 25% Sales (expand team), 15% Operations
- Expected milestones: $2M ARR by month 12, 50+ customers by month 18
- Runway: 24+ months at current burn

**Visual Strategy**: 
Top: Large "$5M Series A" callout  
Middle: Pie chart showing use of funds breakdown  
Bottom: Milestone callout box showing key targets

**Speaker Notes**: 
"We're raising $5M to accelerate our growth. Sixty percent goes to engineering to scale the platform and add new capabilities. Twenty-five percent goes to sales to accelerate our go-to-market. Fifteen percent to operations and infrastructure. This gets us to $2M ARR and establishes market leadership."

**Data Requirements**: 
- Round size: $5M
- Use of funds breakdown (%) and amounts ($)
- Milestone targets by quarter
- Runway calculation

### SLIDE 14: Vision & Impact

Inspiring vision of the future we're creating.

**Supporting Points**:

Full-bleed inspirational visual showing doctors with patients (not with paperwork). Overlay large vision statement. Keep text minimal—this is about emotion and vision.

**Speaker Notes**: 
"But this is bigger than one company. This is about rebuilding healthcare around physicians and patients instead of paperwork. When we succeed, healthcare will have tens of thousands fewer burned-out doctors, and billions of dollars of productivity returned to the system."

## Narrative Flow & Key Transitions

### Opening Hook (Slides 1-2)
Problem is immediately established: doctors are drowning in paperwork, losing time with patients.

### Building Credibility (Slides 3-4)
Solution is clearly presented with actual product demonstration showing it works.

### Proof Points (Slides 5-6)
Traction validates the approach—real revenue growth and customer validation.

### Market Opportunity (Slides 7-8)
Large, growing market with clear business model shows this is worth solving.

### Competitive Advantage (Slides 9-10)
Clear positioning and focused go-to-market strategy shows execution capability.

### Team & Execution (Slides 11-12)
Team credentials and financial projections show ability to capitalize.

### The Ask & Vision (Slides 13-14)
Clear funding request with inspiring vision creates the call to action.

### Close (Slide 15)
Contact information and clear next steps for follow-up.

---

## Key Messaging Points

Reinforce throughout the deck:
1. **Problem**: Physicians are burned out from administrative burden
2. **Solution**: AI automates documentation, freeing doctors for patient care
3. **Proof**: Real revenue growth, real customers, real impact
4. **Opportunity**: $8B market, underserved ambulatory segment
5. **Team**: Healthcare + AI + startup execution expertise
6. **Vision**: Rebuild healthcare around physicians, not paperwork

---

## Emotional Arc

- **Slides 1-2**: Set up the problem (tension)
- **Slide 3-6**: Introduce solution and early wins (relief + validation)
- **Slides 7-10**: Establish opportunity and strategy (confidence building)
- **Slides 11-12**: Build trust through team and numbers (credibility)
- **Slides 13-14**: Create aspiration with funding and vision (inspiration)
- **Slide 15**: Call to action (commitment)
