# Pitch Deck Builder

A comprehensive skill for creating professional pitch decks through conversational discovery, narrative structuring, and technical execution with context-aware best practices.

## 🎯 Overview

This skill guides you through the complete process of creating an effective pitch deck, from initial concept through final presentation. It combines:

- **Conversational discovery** to extract key business insights
- **Narrative arc construction** that resonates with target audiences  
- **Modern slide design** with clarity and visual impact
- **Token-aware chunking strategies** to prevent context overflow
- **Iterative refinement** based on presentation goals

Whether you're pitching to investors, customers, or strategic partners, this skill provides a structured yet flexible workflow for crafting compelling presentations.

## 🚀 When to Use This Skill

Use Pitch Deck Builder when:

- Creating investor pitch decks or fundraising presentations
- Building sales decks or business presentations
- Developing product launch presentations
- Crafting founder or team introductions
- Any scenario requiring strategic narrative presentation of business concepts

## 📋 Core Workflow

### Phase 1: Discovery Interview
We start with a conversational discovery interview to understand your pitch requirements. This prevents wasted effort and ensures the final deck aligns with your goals.

The discovery process covers:
1. **Foundation** - Pitch type, audience, core narrative, stage, and constraints
2. **Business Fundamentals** - Problem, solution, market, business model, traction, competition
3. **Narrative Elements** - Founding story, customer evidence, vision, team, milestones
4. **Data & Materials** - Metrics, visual assets, and must-include elements

### Phase 2: Narrative Planning
After discovery, we create a detailed narrative plan that serves as your deck's blueprint:
- **Slide Sequence** - Recommended structure based on pitch type
- **Content Outline** - Title, core message, supporting points, visuals for each slide
- **Speaker Notes** - Guidance for presenting each slide

### Phase 3: Design & Technical Execution
Once narrative is approved, we execute the technical creation using modern presentation tools and design principles:
- **Design Principles** - Whitespace, typography, color strategy, data visualization
- **Technical Implementation** - HTML/PPTX workflow with consistent styling
- **Chunking Strategy** - For large decks (>15 slides), we build in phases for context management

### Phase 4: Refinement & Delivery
After initial creation, we support iterative improvement:
- Visual review and layout validation
- Content refinement based on feedback
- Design iteration and styling adjustments
- Export preparation and presentation guidance

## 🎨 Design Principles

### Visual Standards
- **Whitespace**: 40-60% per slide for maximum impact
- **Typography**: Large headlines (36-48pt), readable body text (18-24pt)
- **Color Strategy**: 3-4 color palette with brand color, accent, and neutral base
- **Data Visualization**: Charts with clear titles, minimal junk, data-driven insight

### Storytelling Frameworks
We support multiple narrative structures:
- **Hero's Journey** - Classic startup narrative arc
- **Problem-Agitate-Solve** - Sales-focused structure
- **Pixar Pitch** - Simple, powerful narrative template
- **Before-After-Bridge** - Contrast current vs. future state
- **Epiphany Framework** - Unique insight-driven approach

2. Conduct structured discovery interview

5. Validate with thumbnail preview
6. Iterate based on feedback
7. Export final PPTX to outputs folder

### Context Management
For decks >15 slides, we use a phased approach:
- **Phase 1**: Opening slides (Cover through Product) 
- **Phase 2**: Business slides (Market through Competition)
- **Phase 3**: Closing slides (Team through Ask)

Each phase is completed fully, then combined for final result. This ensures every slide receives proper attention without context constraints.

## 📊 Deck Types

### Investor Pitch (Pre-Seed/Seed)
- **Length**: 12-15 slides
- **Focus**: Team, problem, early traction
- **Tone**: Ambitious but realistic
- **Key slides**: Problem, solution, why now, founding story, early metrics

### Investor Pitch (Series A+)
- **Length**: 15-18 slides  
- **Focus**: Growth metrics, business model, competitive moats
- **Tone**: Data-driven, scalable, market-leading
- **Key slides**: Growth charts, unit economics, competitive landscape, market expansion

### Sales/Product Pitch
- **Length**: 10-15 slides
- **Focus**: Customer value, ROI, implementation
- **Tone**: Customer-centric, benefit-oriented, practical
- **Key slides**: Problem/pain point, benefits, customer success, pricing, next steps

### Partnership Pitch
- **Length**: 8-12 slides
- **Focus**: Strategic alignment, mutual value, capabilities
- **Tone**: Collaborative, strategic, win-win
- **Key slides**: Company overview, strategic fit, partnership model, collaboration case

## 🎓 Best Practices

### Discovery Quality
- Listen more than prescribe - let the story emerge naturally
- Ask "why" and "so what" to uncover deeper insights
- Notice what gets emphasized - often the strongest narrative elements
- Validate understanding by reflecting back key points

### Narrative Construction
- Start with why, not what - emotional connection before details
- Use assertive slide titles that make claims, not generic labels
- Place strongest material in middle third of deck (slides 6-9 in a 15-slide deck)
- End with clarity on the ask and vision for impact

### Design Execution
- When in doubt, remove elements rather than add them
- Use data visualization to replace bullet points
- Test readability at thumbnail size - key points should still be clear
- Maintain consistent spacing, alignment, and typography
